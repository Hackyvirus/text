Decision Matrix

To determine profitability on supply of rectified spirit to Liquor manufacturers

Brought to you by the authors of

Why Decision matrix to be used for sale of ENA to liquor manufacturers:

An amendment is made to the levy Section 9 of the Central Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017 (‘the CGST Act’). In terms of the said amendment, rectified spirit, known as un-denatured extra neutral alcohol (‘RS’) supplied for manufacture of alcohol for human consumption (‘Liquor’) is to be kept outside the levy section retrospectively w.e.f. 1.7.2017 i.e. it is exempt from GST. The amendment is made to address the prevalent ambiguity on the taxability or otherwise of RS used in the manufacture of Liquor. However, denatured spirit made from RS continues to be taxable under GST.

While the prices of denatured spirit supplied to the oil marketing companies are fixed for a year under a contract with such companies, the price of the RS is market driven and at times it is substantially higher than the price of denatured spirit. In such a case, a calculated decision of tapping the rectified spirit market will help in optimization of the profit for many cane-based sugar industry having distilleries (‘the Distilleries’).

The decision to sale RS for manufacture of Liquor however should not be taken solely based on higher price as there could be some intrinsic costs to be looked at like reversal of proportionate amount of common input tax credit of inputs, input services and capital goods (‘common ITC’) in terms of the provisions of Rule 42 and Rule 43 of the Central Goods and Services Tax Rules, 2017 on supply of RS to liquor manufacturers. The decision to sale RS to Liquor manufacturers could be profitable when total incremental revenue from sale of RS is higher than the amount of ITC to be reversed.

Primarily, inputs such as molasses, consumables, other raw materials, some capital goods, input services are common between taxable and such exempt supply. The reversal on account of supply of exempt RS will be in addition to the reversal of ITC already being done (Generally the sugar industry has a cogeneration plant, and the electricity supplied by them to the respective electricity boards is exempt from GST). Accordingly, the industry needs to forgo a portion of the ITC available to them, which otherwise was not required to be forgone had such supply of RS been a taxable supply. This could be a huge cost and needs to be properly factored by the Distilleries on sale of RS.

In this regard, Optitax’s has created a decision matrix that indicates the quantum of profit or loss in case any Distillery chooses to explore the option to sell the RS to liquor manufacturers. This decision matrix helps in determining the threshold price as well as quantity of RS to be sold to liquor manufacturers. Ignoring this aspect may prove disastrous if the respective taxpayer generates negative profit as a result of such sale.

We believe that the Indian industry, more particularly the sugar industry operates in most competitive and utmost regulatory environment (regulatory in terms of pricing, stocking, sales, exports, etc.). Hence, choosing an alternative market needs to be a well thought out decision. In this direction Optitax’s decision matrix is our small contribution, particularly because the Sugar Industry, creates more opportunities for everyone and plays major role in creating opportunities in rural India and it’s profitability ultimately adds value to the FARMERS.

This decision matrix requires the User to provide few details such as amount of sales (taxable sales, exempt sales, total ITC, common credit of inputs, inputs services, capital goods) for the respective applicable period of one financial year or part of the year as the case may be. Once it is filled, all the user needs to do is to click the ‘calculate’ button to generate the output values for the amount of profit or loss that the User will incur on proposed sale or discontinuance of sale of RS
